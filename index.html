How this differs from other spectrums

A simple left-right scale packs many kinds of disagreement into one number—markets, tradition, civil liberties, nationalism, and more end up fused. People with similar labels can want very different things.

The familiar two-axis “political compass” (often economic left-right plus an authoritarian-libertarian axis) is a step forward: it separates who should control resources from how much the state should regulate life. Still, one “authority” slider tends to blur together political centralization (who governs and how), economic centralization (markets and inequality versus redistribution), and social centralization (fixed roles and moral order versus voluntary, flat cooperation). This quiz treats those as three separable questions, each with its own pole pair.

The hexagon is a layout choice, not a fourth ideology: your three scores are combined so that moving toward liberty, meritocracy, or hierarchy pulls you toward different corners, while mixed answers land toward the centre. No model captures every debate; this one is meant to make trade-offs between dimensions visible instead of hiding them inside a single spectrum.
